## ğŸ” DISCOVERY QUESTIONS

### **1. Current Setup & Usage (3-4 min)**

**Questions:**
1. "Tell me about your current AI agent setup - what are you using?" 
   - Cursor agents? Other tools?
   - How many agents do you run?
   - Solo dev or team?

2. "Walk me through a typical day with your agents"
   - What tasks do agents handle?
   - How much time do you spend managing them?

3. "How are your agents currently set up?"
   - Manual setup or automated?
   - How long did initial setup take?

**Listen For:**
- Agent count (solo dev: 1-2, team: 5-8+)
- Setup complexity (manual vs automated)
- Time spent on management

---

### **2. Pain Points & Blockers (4-5 min)**

**Questions:**
1. "What's the biggest frustration with your current agent setup?"
   - Let them talk, don't interrupt
   - Follow up: "Tell me more about that"
   - Dig deeper: "What happens when [pain point] occurs?"

2. "Do your agents ever stall or get stuck?"
   - How often?
   - How do you currently handle it?
   - How much time do you lose?

3. "What visibility do you have into agent status?"
   - Do you check manually?
   - Do you get notifications?
   - What's missing?

4. "What takes up most of your time with agents?"
   - Setup?
   - Monitoring?
   - Debugging stalls?
   - Creating workflows?

**Listen For:**
- **Top 3 pain points** (rank them mentally)
- Frequency of problems (daily, weekly, occasional)
- Current workarounds (manual checks, restart, etc.)
- Time lost (hours per week)

---

### **3. Ideal Solution Vision (3-4 min)**

**Questions:**
1. "If you had a magic wand, what would the perfect agent ops tool do?"
   - Let them dream big
   - Don't limit their vision
   - Note: Features they mention vs what we have

2. "What would make this valuable enough to pay for?"
   - What's the ROI they need?
   - Time savings? Reliability? Visibility?

3. "Show me a scenario where this would save you time"
   - Walk through a real example
   - Quantify: "How many hours per week would that save?"

**Listen For:**
- Must-have features (vs nice-to-have)
- Value perception (time saved, peace of mind, etc.)
- Willingness to pay (indirect signals)

---

### **4. Willingness to Pay (2-3 min)**

**Questions:**
1. "Have you paid for agent-related tools before?"
   - What did you pay?
   - What was the value?

2. "What would make sense as a monthly fee for this?"
   - Don't lead with our pricing
   - See what they suggest first
   - Then share our range: "$49-399/mo depending on features"

3. "What about setup - would you pay a one-time setup fee?"
   - How much seems reasonable?
   - Or prefer monthly only?

**Listen For:**
- Price sensitivity (they'll say if too high/low)
- Value anchors (what they've paid before)
- Preferred pricing model (monthly, one-time, usage-based)

---

### **5. Next Steps & Timeline (1-2 min)**

**Questions:**
1. "When would you need something like this?"
   - Urgent (this week)?
   - Soon (this month)?
   - Just exploring?

2. "Would you be interested in being a design partner?"
   - Free/cheap setup ($49/mo first 3 months)
   - In exchange: Case study + feedback
   - Early access to features

3. "What would need to happen for you to say yes?"
   - Any blockers?
   - Questions or concerns?

**Listen For:**
- Urgency (high urgency = better lead)
- Interest level (design partner = high interest)
- Objections (address them)
